Environmental Education & Trail Signage System

ISAT 290| Dynamics Model of Infectious Disease Spread

James Madison University has a exclusive science major called ISAT (Integrated Science And Technology). This major is supposed to be a very broad science major that is that can lead to many different fields such as enviromental sustainability, data science, systems engineering, etc. In ISAT 290, a class focused on modeling feedback loops, my classmates and I created an infection diagram in Stella Architect to model how an infectious disease would realistically behave.

This Stella Architect model simulates the spread of an infectious disease through a population using an extended SEIR (Susceptible, Exposed, Infected, and Recovered) framework. Individuals transition between susceptible, exposed, infected, hospitalized, quarantined, recovered, and deceased states based on contact rates, time delays, and behavioral responses. The model incorporates public health interventions such as vaccination, quarantine, and social distancing, while also tracking cumulative outcomes including total deaths, hospitalizations, and economic costs. By allowing users to adjust intervention parameters and observe resulting feedback loops, the model demonstrates how behavioral and policy decisions influence both health and economic outcomes during an outbreak.

After I collected the GIS data, I developed two primary types of signage: five ecosystem information boards describing distinct ecological zones, and fifteen plant identification signs highlighting native and invasive species throughout the preserve.
